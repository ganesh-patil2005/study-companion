❓ Daily Planner — Help & Guide

Purpose

The Daily Planner lets you plan your study day in advance, log what you actually did, then compare the two to see where you met your targets and where you fell short.

✏ Tab 1 — Daily Study Log

  • Log each study session with subject, chapter, topic, time slot, and questions solved.
  • Use the date picker + arrows to view sessions for any past date.
  • Click Edit on any logged session to modify it.
  • Also save to Learning Book — expand the blue toggle at the bottom of the form to add “My Understanding” and “Key Formula” for the same chapter. Saving will create an entry in the Learning Book automatically — no need to visit the Learning Book page separately. Leave both fields blank to skip.
Tip: Log sessions as you finish them, not at the end of the day. Expand the Learning Book section right after finishing a chapter to capture concepts while they are fresh — one save, two records.

🎯 Tab 2 — Plan Builder

  • Set the date, then add individual task blocks (time slot, subject, chapter, activity, expected questions).
  • Use Clone Plan to copy a previous day's plan as a starting point. Edit as needed, then Save.
  • Each task shows in the staged list on the right. Click Edit or × to modify before saving.
  • Click Save Full Plan to lock the plan for that date.
Tip: Build tomorrow's plan the night before. Even a rough plan is better than no plan.

📊 Tab 3 — Plan vs Actual (most important)

Select a date to see the full comparison. The report shows:

  • Summary metrics — planned vs actual: time, questions, accuracy.
  • Completion Rate — % of planned tasks that were actually executed (matched by subject + chapter + activity).
  • Subject Coverage — how many planned tasks per subject were done.
  • Task-level comparison:
    • ✅ Done — A matching actual session was logged for this planned task.
    • ⚡ Partial — A session was logged but questions or time were significantly lower than planned.
    • ❌ Missed — No actual session found matching this planned task.
  • Bonus Sessions — sessions you logged that were not in the plan (unplanned extra work).
  • Accuracy Gap — difference between your planned accuracy target and what you actually achieved.
How to use it: Review last night’s plan vs actual every morning. Any missed tasks should be rescheduled. Any subject consistently missed means your time allocation is off — revise the plan.
Tip: A completion rate above 70% is excellent. Below 50% means your plan is too ambitious or you have distractions to address.
