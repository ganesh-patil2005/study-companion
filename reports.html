❓ Reports — Help & Guide

What are Reports?

The Reports page analyses all your data across every section of the app and shows you patterns, strengths, weak spots and gaps — so you can make smarter study decisions.

Note: Reports show live data. The more you log in Learning, Practice, Mistakes, Formula and Revision, the more useful and accurate your reports become.
📊 Subject Overview

Readiness score per subject. Shows accuracy, mistake resolution and revision completion combined into one score.

⚠️ Weak Chapters

Ranks all chapters by lowest accuracy. Top 3 weakest are highlighted. Use this to decide what to study next.

⭐ Mistake Patterns

Breaks down your mistakes by type (Concept, Calculation, etc.) and subject. Shows unresolved mistakes to review.

🔁 Revision Map

Shows which chapters have been revised, which are overdue, and which have never been revised despite having notes.

🧠 Mind Map

Chapter-by-chapter view of your notes, formulas, memory tricks and mistakes — all in one place. Best for quick revision.

⏱️ Time & Effort

Total study time, sessions, accuracy and journal streak. Shows where you are investing your time.

📊 Subject Overview — How to Read It

  • Practice Accuracy bar — % of questions answered correctly across all sessions for that subject.
  • Mistake Resolution bar — % of logged mistakes marked as Revised. Low = you have open mistakes to review.
  • Revision Completion bar — % of revision sessions marked as Completed for that subject.
  • Readiness Score = 40% accuracy + 30% mistake resolution + 30% revision completion.
Action: Any subject with Readiness below 40 (red) needs immediate attention — prioritise it in your next study session.

⚠️ Weak Chapters — How to Read It

  • Chapters sorted by accuracy % — weakest at the top.
  • Top 3 weakest rows are highlighted in gold.
  • Pending Mistakes column shows unresolved mistakes for that chapter.
  • Use the Subject filter to focus on one subject at a time.
Action: Any chapter with accuracy below 40% (red badge) should be revised in the Learning Book and re-practised. Schedule it in the Revision Planner.

⭐ Mistake Patterns — How to Read It

  • By Type chart — which kind of mistake you make most. Concept errors = go back to theory. Calculation errors = slow down and be more careful. Careless = check your work before submitting.
  • By Subject chart — which subject has the most unresolved mistakes.
  • Top Chapters table — chapters with the highest mistake count.
  • Pending Mistakes list — recent mistakes you still need to resolve, shown with the correct concept.
Action: Before any test, read all pending mistakes. Mark them Revised only after solving a similar question correctly without help.

🔁 Revision Map — How to Read It

  • Overdue rows (red background) = revision was scheduled for a past date but still not Completed.
  • Revision Gaps section at the bottom = chapters you have learning notes for but have never scheduled a revision for. These are high-risk forgotten topics.
  • Use the Subject filter to plan one subject at a time.
Action: Schedule revisions for all Gap chapters. The forgetting curve means unrevised topics lose 80% retention within a week.
Ideal revision schedule: Day 1, Day 7, Day 30, Day 90 after first studying a topic.

🧠 Mind Map — How to Use It

  • Select a subject tab at the top.
  • Click any chapter to expand it — shows everything you know about that chapter in one place.
  • Sections inside each chapter:
    • My Understanding — your notes from the Learning Book.
    • Key Formulas (from notes) — formulas entered in the Learning Book formula field.
    • Formula Book — entries from the Formula Book with ⭐ for favourites and yellow memory tricks.
    • Mistakes — all mistakes for that chapter with correct concept shown inline.
Best use: Open Mind Map the day before a test for a chapter. Everything you need for last-minute revision is in one scrollable view — no switching between pages.
Tip: The more you fill in the Learning Book, Formula Book and Mistake Book, the richer and more useful your Mind Map becomes.

⏱️ Time & Effort — How to Read It

  • Total study time — sum of all time values entered in the Practice Book (in hours).
  • Journal Streak — consecutive days with a Success Journal entry. A streak shows consistency.
  • Difficulty breakdown — shows if you are practising enough Hard questions, or only staying in the Easy zone.
  • Time by Subject — shows if you are giving enough time to weaker subjects.
  • Accuracy by Subject — weakest subject shown first.
Action: If time for a weak subject is low and accuracy is also low — you need to dedicate more sessions to it. If time is high but accuracy is still low — the problem is approach, not effort. Go back to notes and theory.
